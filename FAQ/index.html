How long can Bambu flooring last?
Bambu flooring can last up to 50 years if the bamboo flooring is well maintained. The average lifespan of bamboo flooring is approximately 20 to 25 years.
water_drop
Are Bambu flooring water resistant?
Yes, bamboo flooring is known for its resistance to wear and tear. The natural hardness of bamboo, along with additional treatments like carbonization, contributes to its durability, making it a resilient and long-lasting flooring option

Note: just like other construction supplies, bamboo is not 100% waterproof
bug_report
Are Bambu products termite-resistant?
Yes, Bambu products are termite-resistant. Through a carbonization/heating process, our items undergo treatment that eliminates starch, sugar, and other components known to attract termites

Note: just like other construction supplies, bamboo is not 100% termite proof

Are bamboo flooring suitable for kitchens and bathrooms?
Yes, Bambu bamboo flooring is well-suited for kitchens and bathrooms, designed to withstand fluctuations in humidity and provide a durable flooring solution.

How does bamboo compare to traditional hardwood?
Bambu flooring offers exceptional durability, surpassing traditional hardwood. Bamboo's inherent natural hardness not only makes it an excellent choice for high-traffic areas but also positions it as a superior option for longevity.
star
Are Bambu products easily scratched?
Bambu flooring is crafted from premium materials, specifically using 5-7-year-old matured Mao/Moso bamboo, known for its exceptional density and durability, making it ideal for flooring. Bambu flooring surpasses Narra in hardness, resulting in superior scratch resistance
volunteer_activism
How to maintain bamboo flooring?
Bambu bamboo flooring is low-maintenance. Regular sweeping or vacuuming and occasional damp mopping are usually sufficient.
eco
What makes Bambu flooring a sustainable choice?
Bambu bamboo flooring is crafted from rapidly renewable bamboo, which grows much faster than traditional hardwoods. Our harvesting methods are sustainable, ensuring minimal impact on the environment.
